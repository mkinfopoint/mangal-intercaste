Inter Caste Marriage

The Intercaste Marriage Scheme is a government initiative facilitated by the Social Justice Department to encourage and support marriages between individuals from different castes. The scheme aims to promote integration, equality, and social harmony by providing various forms of assistance and benefits to inter-caste couples.

The financial support offered under the Intercaste Marriage Scheme may include subsidies, monetary assistance, or tax incentives. The exact nature and amount of financial benefits depend on the government's policies and guidelines. This support is designed to ease the financial burden on inter-caste couples and promote their overall well-being.

1.Collect Documents:
  • Marriage certificate registered under Hindu Marriage Act or Special Marriage Act.
  • Proof of residence for both bride and groom.
  • Caste certificates for both bride and groom.
  • Income certificates (if applicable).
  • Educational certificates (if applicable).
  • Passport-size photographs of both bride and groom.
2.Verification Process:
  • The department will verify the submitted documents and eligibility criteria.
  • This process may take some time, so patience is key.
3.Disbursement of Benefits:
  • Upon successful verification, the financial benefits will be disbursed through ECS or other designated channels.
  • You will receive a notification about the disbursement.

Register Marriage

Registering a marriage in Tamil Nadu without a formal wedding ceremony, often referred to as a "court marriage" under the Special Marriage Act

Registering a marriage in Tamil Nadu without the involvement or consent of parents is legally possible and follows a defined process under the Special Marriage Act, 1954. This process is particularly useful for couples who might face familial or societal opposition.

1.Notice of Intended Marriage:
  • Submit Notice: Both parties must submit a 'Notice of Intended Marriage' to the Marriage Registrar of the district where at least one of the parties has resided for a minimum of 30 days prior to the notice.
  • Public Notice: The notice will be published publicly for 30 days at the Registrar's office.
2.Objection Period:
  • Objections: During the 30-day period, any person can object to the marriage if they have valid reasons under the Act.
  • No Objections: If no objections are raised, the marriage can proceed.
3.Declaration and Signing:
  • Declaration: After the 30-day notice period, the couple and three witnesses must sign a declaration in front of the Marriage Registrar.
  • Marriage Solemnization: The marriage can be solemnized at the office of the Marriage Registrar or at a location within a reasonable distance.
4.Marriage Certificate:
  • Issuance of Certificate: The Marriage Registrar will issue the marriage certificate after the solemnization of the marriage.
  • Legal Proof: The marriage certificate serves as legal proof of the marriage.
